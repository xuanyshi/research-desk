Changelog

How this site grew since it launched on 2026-07-06 — 15 milestones.

  1. 2026-07-22

    Deep read: the argument framework for healthcare evidence

    Published a bilingual deep read and guide for the 2024 Nature Medicine Comment on the argument framework. The page compares measurement-centered synthesis with conclusion-and-premise argumentation, uses precision oncology and community masking to show how heterogeneous evidence supports or defeats claims, and separates epistemic argument arrows from causal-DAG relations.

  2. 2026-07-22

    Deep read: life-course evidence on secondhand smoke

    Published a bilingual deep read and public guide for the 2026 Nature Human Behaviour umbrella review of secondhand smoke, covering 111 systematic reviews, 130 health outcomes, and 226 independent associations. The page separates fetal exposure to maternal active smoking from environmental secondhand smoke and reports Evidence class, GRADE, dose response, and observational bias side by side.

  3. 2026-07-21

    Thirteen DAG and evidence-synthesis deep reads

    Removed 13 duplicate studies from the newly added local PDFs and published bilingual deep reads and public reading guides for 13 unique papers, spanning DAG construction and reporting, expert consensus, causal-reasoning benchmarks, knowledge-graph evaluation, umbrella reviews, and clinical applications. Each page separates structural stability, predictive performance, expert agreement, epidemiological credibility, and causal-effect evidence.

  4. 2026-07-20

    Structured deep-read system

    All 25 bilingual deep reads now include a 30-second takeaway, study design, evidence strength, key statistics, reading priorities, links to original figures and a five-step reading path. The full analyses were also revised for argument structure, number formatting and keyword emphasis, supporting both rapid review and close examination.

  5. 2026-07-10

    Topic discovery expanded to OpenAlex

    Added topic-based OpenAlex discovery alongside the journal whitelist and preprint feeds, extending coverage to formally published research beyond the whitelist. Results are deduplicated across sources by DOI and then screened with the existing relevance scoring process.

  6. 2026-07-10

    Combined disease and topic filters

    Unified filtering across the library and deep reads: disease and topic tags can be selected in combination with keyword, source, status and score criteria. The controlled tag vocabulary was also normalized to reduce duplicate classification of the same concept.

  7. 2026-07-09

    Public-release safety gate

    Moved public-content redaction into a dedicated module and added a pre-deployment scan of the generated site. A release is blocked when unpublished research information is detected, with regression tests and the publishing gate jointly enforcing the public boundary.

  8. 2026-07-08

    Journal metric badges

    Every journal article now carries its CAS division, JCR quartile and latest impact factor (from the offline ShowJCR dataset); preprints are tagged arXiv / medRxiv. Badges show across the library, deep reads and daily digest, with the journal name too.

  9. 2026-07-07

    Library tiers + one-year backfill

    A one-off backfill of the past year: 42 most-relevant papers (score ≥9) into the library, 347 archived by month into the digest. The library visually and by filter separates hand-picked entries from machine-backfilled candidates.

  10. 2026-07-07

    arXiv + medRxiv sources

    Added arXiv and medRxiv alongside journals — where the LLM/knowledge-graph causal-DAG methodology papers actually live, off the journal whitelist.

  11. 2026-07-07

    Scoring refocused

    Scoring narrowed to causal-DAG construction methodology (e.g. Living DAG, ESC-DAG and related literature-to-DAG work), evidence triangulation, and AI/LLM/KG-adjacent work; pure target-trial-emulation clinical applications weighted down.

  12. 2026-07-07

    LLM scoring wired up

    New papers are scored for relevance and given a short summary by an LLM (DeepSeek), against the stated interests; the wording is neutral third person, written for readers.

  13. 2026-07-07

    Public sharing layer

    Switched to dual-repo publishing: a private source repo runs the data and pipeline, a public repo serves the site. Added an RSS feed, social share cards and a sitemap for colleagues to follow and share.

  14. 2026-07-07

    Journal tracking in place

    Weekday tracking from a whitelist of strong journals (via Crossref), filtering out society-meeting abstracts and boosting AI-adjacent work.

  15. 2026-07-06

    Site launched

    The personal literature-tracking site went live. Dropped MkDocs for a fully custom, bilingual magazine-style static site, with a fixed seven-part deep-read framework.
